There is a specific feeling when you walk through the gates of Southwest University Park. The smell of local street tacos, the view of the downtown skyline over the left-field wall, and the sound of the "Bark Back" from the stands—it’s a sensory experience that defines the Sun City.

In El Paso, baseball season isn't just about the box score; it’s our favorite social hour. Whether you’re a die-hard stats fan or just there for the nachos and the atmosphere, the 2026 season is officially in full swing. Here is your "Live Like a Local" roadmap to the ballpark this month.

## The May Lineup: Home Games & Must-See Events

If you are planning your May calendar, these are the dates that locals have circled:

* 🎆 May 9 | Fireworks Spectacular: If you want to see the best view in the city, stay after the final out on Saturday night. The post-game fireworks show against the backdrop of the downtown buildings is a local rite of passage.
* 🍌 May 15-16 | Banana Ball Comes to El Paso: The world-famous Banana Ball is taking over the city as the Texas Tailgaters face off against the Firefighters. It’s high-speed, high-entertainment, and purely unpredictable. If you missed the ticket lottery, keep a close eye on local resale groups!  

## The "Local’s" Game Day Strategy

How to navigate the ballpark like you’ve lived here for decades:

* The $5.55 Secret: Wednesday games are "Seniors Eat Free" days (for the 60+ crowd), but for everyone else, look for the midweek afternoon "Business Person Specials." It’s the ultimate excuse to leave the office early and catch some sunshine.
* The Streetcar Hack: Downtown parking can be a mission during home stands. Avoid the headache by parking for free near the Glory Road Transit Center and taking the El Paso Streetcar directly to the stadium. It’s free, air-conditioned, and drops you right at the gate.
* The Sunday Tradition: Bringing the family? After every Sunday home game, the "Little Pups" (kids 12 and under) get to run the bases. It’s the perfect cap to a family weekend.

## Live Like a Local: Pro-Tips

* Military Appreciation: We are proud of our Fort Bliss community. Every Sunday through Thursday game, active-duty military and veterans can save up to 25% on tickets via GovX or by showing an ID at the box office.
* Hydration Hero: May in El Paso is beautiful, but the sun is no joke. If you’re sitting on the first-base side, you’ll be in the sun until about the 4th inning—bring your shades and a hat!
* The Food Pick: Skip the standard dog and head to the Huahua Hills stand for the loaded "Nacho Helmet." It’s a shareable local staple and a ballpark icon.
