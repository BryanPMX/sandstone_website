# Can I Buy a Home Before Arriving in El Paso? A PCS Buyer's Guide (2026)

**Meta Description:** Yes, you can buy a home in El Paso before your PCS arrival. Learn how VA loan pre-approval, spouse occupancy, and remote closing work for Fort Bliss buyers in 2026.

---

One of the most common questions from service members with incoming orders to Fort Bliss is whether they can buy a home before they physically arrive in El Paso. The short answer is **yes** — and for many military families, starting the process before arrival is actually the smarter move.

Here's exactly how it works.

---

## Yes, You Can Buy Before You Arrive — Here's Why

The VA loan program was built with military realities in mind. You don't need to be physically present in El Paso to start the homebuying process, make an offer, or even close on a property. Two key provisions make this possible:

**1. PCS Orders satisfy the occupancy requirement**
The VA requires borrowers to intend to occupy the home as a primary residence. For active-duty service members, providing your official PCS orders demonstrates that intent — even if your report date is weeks or months away. As long as you can provide official orders, the VA and your lender will not hold you to a strict 60-day occupancy rule. Your move-in date should align with your report date.

**2. Spouse occupancy counts**
The VA considers the service member's spouse to be a proxy for occupancy purposes. If your spouse moves into the new home before you arrive, the occupancy requirement is officially met. This lets your family get settled, enroll children in school, and set up the household while you complete out-processing and travel.

---

## Step 1: Get VA Pre-Approved Before You Arrive

The first step — and the most important one — is getting pre-approved for your VA loan before your PCS date. VA pre-approval is based on your financial documents and your Certificate of Eligibility (COE), not your physical presence in El Paso. You can complete the entire pre-approval process online or by phone before your PCS.

The process takes 1 to 5 business days with the right lender and documents. Starting before you arrive means you can begin shopping homes virtually and make offers immediately — or even remotely — the moment you find the right property.

**Documents you'll need for pre-approval:**
- DD-214 or current military ID
- Last 2 years of W-2s or LES statements
- Last 2 months of bank statements
- Certificate of Eligibility (COE) — your lender can often pull this electronically in minutes
- Copy of PCS orders

## Step 3: Tour Homes Virtually

Not being in El Paso is no longer a barrier to buying. Most El Paso agents experienced with military buyers offer:

- **Live video walkthroughs** via FaceTime or Zoom
- **3D Matterport tours** on listing pages
- **Inspection reports** shared digitally with detailed photos
- **Power of Attorney (POA)** for closing — your spouse or a designated representative can sign on your behalf at closing

The entire process can be managed from another state by using a specific Power of Attorney for the closing and relying on your real estate agent for virtual walkthroughs and inspections.

## Step 4: Know the VA Appraisal Process in El Paso

Before your loan closes, the home must pass a VA appraisal. This step verifies both the market value and that the property meets the VA's **Minimum Property Requirements (MPRs)** for safety, sanitation, and structural soundness.

In El Paso, the VA appraisal panel typically turns around in 8 to 12 days — faster than many other Texas markets. Common local issues include sun-damaged roofs, outdated HVAC systems, termites, and drainage problems. A pre-inspection before the official VA appraisal is a smart move — it catches issues early and reduces the chance of delays.

VA appraisals in El Paso range from $800 to $1,150 depending on property size. Texas requires termite inspections in El Paso — expect $50–$150 for this service.

## What About VA Loan Limits?

If you have full VA loan entitlement — meaning no other active VA loans — there is no county loan limit. You can borrow above conforming limits without a down payment. El Paso County's conforming loan limit for 2026 is $806,500, which is well above the median home price in the area, meaning most Fort Bliss buyers have full flexibility.

If you currently have a VA loan on another property, a **second-tier entitlement** may allow you to carry two VA loans simultaneously. Speak with a VA-approved lender about your specific situation.

## What If You Need More Time After Closing?

For active-duty service members with PCS orders, the VA's 60-day occupancy timeline is officially extended. Your move-in date should align with your report date. The key is clear communication with your lender and providing the documentation to support your timeline.

In practice, most VA lenders in El Paso are experienced with PCS scenarios and will not flag a delayed move-in as long as your orders clearly show your report date.

## Common Questions About Buying Before Arrival

**Can I make an offer without seeing the home in person?**
Yes. With virtual tours, a local agent, and a pre-inspection report, most military buyers in El Paso make confident offers remotely. Your agent becomes your eyes on the ground.

**Can my spouse close on the home without me?**
Yes, with a specific Power of Attorney (POA). Your real estate attorney or lender can prepare the document and your spouse can sign at closing on your behalf.

**Will sellers in El Paso accept VA offers?**
In El Paso's market — where Fort Bliss generates a substantial portion of buyer activity — VA loans are well understood and routinely accepted by sellers and listing agents.

**What if the home doesn't appraise at the purchase price?**
VA loans include an escape clause that allows you to walk away if the home fails or appraises below the purchase price. You can also negotiate the price down or request the seller make necessary repairs.
