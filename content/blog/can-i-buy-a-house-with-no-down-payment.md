## Can I Buy a House With No Down Payment?

If you've been dreaming of homeownership but haven't saved enough for a traditional down payment, you're not alone — and you're not out of options. The good news is that **buying a house with no down payment is entirely possible** through specific loan programs designed for eligible buyers.

---

## 1. Yes, You Can — With the Right Loan Program

Several government-backed mortgage programs allow qualified buyers to purchase a home with 0% down. These programs were created specifically to remove the biggest barrier to homeownership: the upfront cost. If you meet the eligibility requirements, you could move into your new home without putting a single dollar down.

---

## 2. VA Loans: The Best Zero-Down Option for Veterans

If you are an active-duty service member, veteran, or eligible surviving spouse, a **VA loan** is the most powerful no-down-payment mortgage available.

**Key benefits of VA loans:**
- 0% down payment required
- No private mortgage insurance (PMI)
- Competitive interest rates
- Flexible credit score requirements

The VA loan is backed by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs, making it one of the safest and most affordable paths to homeownership for those who have served.

---

## 3. USDA Loans: Zero Down for Rural and Suburban Homes

The **USDA Rural Development loan** allows buyers in eligible rural and suburban areas to finance 100% of the home's purchase price with no down payment.

**Who qualifies for a USDA loan?**
- Buyers purchasing in a USDA-designated rural or suburban area
- Households meeting income limits (typically moderate to low income)
- Buyers with a reasonable credit history

Use the USDA's official property eligibility map to check if the home you want qualifies. Many suburban areas outside major cities are eligible — more than most people expect.

---

## 4. Down Payment Assistance Programs Can Cover Your Costs

Even if you don't qualify for a VA or USDA loan, you may still achieve zero out-of-pocket costs through **Down Payment Assistance (DPA) programs**.

These programs — offered by state housing agencies, counties, and nonprofits — provide:
- **Grants** that do not need to be repaid
- **Forgivable loans** that are forgiven after living in the home a set number of years
- **Deferred loans** with no monthly payments until you sell or refinance

DPA programs are commonly paired with FHA loans and are especially available to first-time homebuyers and buyers with moderate incomes.

---

## 5. What to Consider Before Buying With No Down Payment

While buying with no down payment is a powerful option, it's important to understand the trade-offs:

- **Higher monthly payments** — financing 100% of the purchase price means a larger loan balance
- **More interest paid over time** — a higher loan amount means more interest accumulated
- **No immediate equity** — you start with zero equity, which matters if home values dip
- **Some programs require mortgage insurance** — FHA loans, for example, require an upfront and annual mortgage insurance premium

That said, for many buyers, entering the market sooner outweighs waiting years to save a down payment — especially in markets where home prices continue to rise.
